Everyday Things, Hidden Costs

How common items fuel deforestation and forest degradation

Toilet Paper & Paper Towels
27,000 trees are cut down daily for toilet paper production.

Alternative: choose recycled or bamboo-based options instead.

Cardboard Packaging
Increased popularity of online shopping has led to increased demand for cardboard, contributing to deforestation. These packaging often end up in landfills.

Alternative: Shop in store and reuse boxes when possible.

Books, Notebooks & Paper
Traditional paper production relies heavily on virgin wood pulp, leading to deforestation. The paper industry is one of the top consumers of energy and water.

Alternative: Use digital formats, recycled paper, or products certified by the Forest Stewardship Council (FSC).

Chocolate
Cocoa farming is a major driver of deforestation in West Africa, with approximately 50% of protected forests in Côte d'Ivoire cleared for cocoa cultivation. Deforestation for cocoa threatens wildlife habitats and contributes to climate change.

Alternative: Choose chocolate products certified by Rainforest Alliance or Fairtrade.

Fast Fashion
The fashion industry causes 8% of global emissions and 20% of global wastewater, with cotton cultivation linked to deforestation in regions like Brazil's Cerrado..

Alternative: Choose thrift, repair, or buy from sustainable brands.

Palm Oil in Food & Cosmetics
Palm oil production is a leading cause of deforestation in Indonesia, with illegal clearing occurring in protected areas like the Rawa Singkil Wildlife Reserve. This threatens endangered species and contributes to greenhouse gas emissions.

Alternative: Support products with sustainable palm oil certifications (e.g., RSPO) or those that are palm oil-free

Beef
Cattle ranching accounts for 80% of deforestation in the Amazon rainforest.

Alternative: Reducing beef consumption can significantly lower forest destruction and emissions.

Coffee
The shift from shade-grown to sun-grown coffee has led to deforestation, particularly in regions like El Salvador. 2.5 million acres of forest have been cleared for coffee farming globally.

Alternative: Buy shade-grown, bird-friendly, or organic certified coffee.

Wooden Furniture
Unsustainable logging practices, especially for tropical hardwoods like mahogany and teak, lead to significant forest loss. Illegal logging contributes to 50–90% of tropical deforestation in some countries.

Alternative: Purchase FSC-certified furniture, reclaimed wood products, or secondhand items.

Soy
Soy farming is a leading cause of deforestation, especially in South America.

Alternative: Most soy is used as animal feed. Reducing meat consumption lowers soy-driven deforestation.
